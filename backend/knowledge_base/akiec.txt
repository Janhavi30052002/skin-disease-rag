Actinic Keratosis / Intraepithelial Carcinoma (AKIEC)

Actinic keratosis is a precancerous skin lesion caused by cumulative ultraviolet radiation exposure. Some lesions may progress to squamous cell carcinoma.

Typical characteristics:
- Rough or scaly patches
- Red, pink, or skin-colored lesions
- Frequently found on sun-exposed areas
- Persistent texture changes

Clinical notes:
Actinic keratoses are considered early warning signs of skin damage and should be monitored carefully.

Risk factors:
- Long-term sun exposure
- Older age
- Fair skin
- Immunosuppression

Professional evaluation is recommended because some lesions can become invasive cancers.
