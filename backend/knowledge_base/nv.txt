Melanocytic Nevus (NV)

Melanocytic nevus is a common benign skin lesion formed by a collection of melanocytes, the cells responsible for producing pigment in the skin.

Typical characteristics:
- Symmetric appearance
- Uniform color and pigmentation
- Well-defined borders
- Usually small and stable over time

Clinical notes:
Melanocytic nevi are generally non-cancerous and are commonly referred to as moles. Most nevi do not require treatment unless they show suspicious changes.

Warning signs:
- Change in size
- Change in shape
- Change in color
- Bleeding or itching

Medical evaluation is recommended if significant changes are observed.
