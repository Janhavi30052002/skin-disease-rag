Dermatofibroma (DF)

Dermatofibroma is a benign skin growth composed of fibrous tissue. It commonly develops on the extremities and is generally harmless.

Typical characteristics:
- Firm texture
- Small, round nodule
- Brown, pink, or skin-colored appearance
- Dimple sign when pinched

Clinical notes:
Dermatofibromas often arise after minor skin injury, insect bites, or inflammation.

Symptoms:
- Usually asymptomatic
- Occasionally tender or itchy

These lesions are benign and rarely require treatment unless symptomatic or cosmetically concerning.
