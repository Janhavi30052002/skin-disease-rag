Melanoma (MEL)

Melanoma is a malignant skin cancer that develops from melanocytes. It is one of the most serious forms of skin cancer due to its potential to spread to other parts of the body.

Typical characteristics:
- Asymmetry
- Irregular or poorly defined borders
- Multiple colors or uneven pigmentation
- Diameter larger than common moles
- Evolution or change over time

Clinical notes:
Early detection greatly improves treatment outcomes. Melanoma may appear anywhere on the skin and can sometimes arise from existing moles.

ABCDE Rule:
A - Asymmetry
B - Border irregularity
C - Color variation
D - Diameter greater than 6 mm
E - Evolving appearance

Prompt medical assessment is recommended when melanoma is suspected.
