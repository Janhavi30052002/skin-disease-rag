Benign Keratosis-like Lesion (BKL)

Benign keratosis-like lesions include seborrheic keratoses, solar lentigines, and lichenoid keratoses. These lesions are generally non-cancerous.

Typical characteristics:
- Waxy or rough texture
- Well-demarcated borders
- Variable pigmentation
- Raised appearance in some cases

Clinical notes:
These lesions are common in older adults and often develop due to aging or long-term sun exposure.

Symptoms:
- Usually painless
- May occasionally become irritated

Although benign, some lesions can resemble skin cancer and may require professional evaluation.
