Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)

Basal cell carcinoma is the most common form of skin cancer. It arises from basal cells located in the deepest layer of the epidermis.

Typical characteristics:
- Pearly or shiny appearance
- Pink or flesh-colored lesion
- Visible blood vessels
- Slow growth
- May ulcerate over time

Clinical notes:
Basal cell carcinoma rarely spreads to distant organs but can cause significant local tissue damage if left untreated.

Risk factors:
- Chronic sun exposure
- Fair skin
- Older age
- History of skin cancer

Early diagnosis and treatment are important for optimal outcomes.
