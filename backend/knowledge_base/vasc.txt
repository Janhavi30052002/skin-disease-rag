Vascular Lesion (VASC)

Vascular lesions are benign abnormalities involving blood vessels within the skin. Examples include angiomas and other vascular growths.

Typical characteristics:
- Red, purple, or bluish coloration
- Smooth surface
- Often round or oval
- May blanch under pressure

Clinical notes:
Most vascular lesions are harmless and do not require treatment unless they cause cosmetic concerns or symptoms.

Common types:
- Cherry angiomas
- Hemangiomas
- Other benign vascular proliferations

Medical evaluation may be useful if rapid growth or unusual changes occur.
